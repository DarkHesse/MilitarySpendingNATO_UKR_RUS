3 Introduction

Certain voices in the political arena paint a picture of Russia as a peaceful state, that is the victim of US/NATO/EU aggression. In this Notebook I will not address the second of the statement, even though I do believe it is both wrong and very problematic. I am aiming to show however, that the first half of the statement is wrong. Looking at the involvement of the Russian Armed Forces shows, that Russia is a rather aggressive player on the world stage. 5 Timeline and Powers of Russian Presidents

The Russian Constitution makes the President the Supreme Commander in Chief of the Russian Armed Forces. The graph above is somewhat misleading, though. There was no real change of power during Medvedevs term of office as president. Medvedev was very much Putins puppet. De facto Putin has been calling the shots since the late Yeltsin administration.

6 Timeline and locations of the conflicts

First of all: Orange shows ongoing conflicts.

Quite a few conflicts happened on what is considered to be Russian territory. Hence Russia is shown here as a conflict location.

Every conflict from the Second Chechen war on saw Putin as either Prime Minister or President. In his second term as president, Russia seems to have taken to engaging in conflicts that do not happen on former Soviet territory (Central African Republic, Syria, Mali) or on Russian soil. Russia also seems to be more willing to involve itself in conflicts, that see a participation of its political rivals (USA, NATO, EU). More importantly: Russia under Putin has been more than willing, to attack neighbouring states, that were seeking to align themselves with the West (Georgia, Ukraine). Russia also showed a tendency to commit war crimes, as both Syria and the Ukraine show.

7 Bottom lines

  • Putin has officially/unofficially been Commander in Chief of the Russian Armed Forces since 1999.
  • Under his presidency Russia has involved itself in conflicts abroad.
  • This includes conflicts, that involve some level of participation by the USA, NATO, and the EU.
  • It also includes wars of aggression against sovereign Nations.

8 Conclusion

I believe, that it is fair to say, that Russia under Putin has been following a more aggressive course in foreign affairs. This undermines the narrative by some that Russia is a peaceful state, only seeking trade and cooperation. Much like everyone else, Russia is looking out for what it deems its national interests.
